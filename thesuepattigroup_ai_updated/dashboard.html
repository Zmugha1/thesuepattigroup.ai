Sample Situation: Multiple Offer Strategy

SITUATION
My seller at 891 Hillside just got three offers. Two at asking ($589K), one $10K over with a 45-day close. The over-asking buyer has 10% down. The asking-price buyers have 20% down and are pre-approved with TJ. Which should I recommend?
Agent 1: Market Analyst
Recommends: asking-price buyer with TJ
Higher down payment = cleaner close, lower risk of appraisal gap. TJ knows his buyers. 10% down buyer is 30% more likely to have financing issues based on industry data.
Agent 2: Negotiator
Recommends: counter all three
Three offers = leverage. Counter at $599K with escalation clauses. Let buyers compete. Seller nets $10K+ more with minimal risk.
Agent 3: Risk Analyst
Recommends: over-asking if appraisal gap covered
$10K over is attractive, but only if buyer waives appraisal gap. Ask listing agent to request proof of funds and gap coverage before accepting.
Agent 4: Relationship Manager
Recommends: asking-price buyer + thank you to others
TJ is your referral partner. Choosing his buyer strengthens that relationship long-term. The other two buyers stay in your database as warm leads.
CHAIRMAN'S DECISION. JASON APPROVES
Take asking-price offer with TJ's buyer. Send professional decline + thank you to the other two with note: "will keep you in mind for similar listings." Preserves relationships, minimizes risk, strengthens TJ partnership.
