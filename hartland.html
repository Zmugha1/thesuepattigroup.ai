Why Hartland

Hartland is the heart of Lake Country. A village of about 9,500 anchored by a walkable historic downtown, the Bark River winding through, and one of the strongest school districts in the state. Capitol Drive runs through the center of town with locally-owned restaurants, the Hartland Public Library, and small businesses that families have loved for decades.

Hartland sits at a sweet spot in Waukesha County. You get the small-town feel without sacrificing access. I-94 is a 10-minute drive south. Milwaukee is 30 minutes east. Madison is 90 minutes west. The Glacial Drumlin State Trail runs through town for biking and walking. Pine Lake is on the east side, popular for swimming and small-craft boating.

Hartland homes range from charming historic homes in the village (typically $300K-$500K) to newer construction in subdivisions north and west of downtown (typically $450K-$800K), to Pine Lake area homes and larger estate properties on multi-acre lots ($800K and up). Hartland Schools

Hartland is part of the Arrowhead Union High School District, one of Wisconsin's top-rated public school districts. Three elementary feeder districts serve the area:

Hartland-Lakeside School District: Serves North Shore, Lake Country East, and the village proper. Schools include North Shore Middle and Hartland-Lakeside Elementary.

Lake Country School District: Single K-8 school in Hartland. Highly regarded, often referenced as the "small school option."

Swallow School District: Single K-8 school, smaller and more rural feel. Very strong test scores.

For private school, University Lake School in Hartland offers K-12 college prep, and Catholic Memorial High School is in nearby Waukesha.

Hartland Neighborhoods

Downtown Hartland (Village): Historic homes, walkable to Capitol Drive shops and restaurants. Charm and character. Mid-priced.

Pine Lake area: East side, near the small Pine Lake. Homes range from modest ranches to larger lakefront estates. Quieter than downtown.

North Hartland subdivisions: Newer construction. Larger lots. Family-oriented neighborhoods with newer schools. Premium pricing.

West Hartland / countryside: Larger acreage, sometimes with horse-friendly zoning. Fewer homes but distinctive.
