Why Dousman

Dousman gives you space. Where Hartland and Pewaukee feel like established Lake Country villages, Dousman feels like the country, while still being inside it. The village proper has a small downtown, but most of the housing inventory is on larger lots: half-acre, full-acre, and multi-acre properties are common. Buyers come to Dousman when they want elbow room, often with a barn, a pole shed, a workshop, or some land for animals.

Dousman sits on the western edge of Waukesha County with quick access to the Kettle Moraine State Forest Southern Unit, one of Wisconsin's most popular hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing destinations. I-94 is a 10-minute drive south. Milwaukee is about 35 minutes east. Madison is about 60 minutes west.

Dousman Real Estate Market

Dousman pricing varies more than most Lake Country markets because of the lot-size variation. Smaller lot single-family homes in the village proper typically range from $300K to $500K. Larger lot homes (1+ acre) typically range from $450K to $750K, often with outbuildings and rural character. Estate properties on multi-acre lots can run from $700K to over $1M.

Dousman Schools

Dousman is part of the Kettle Moraine School District, which serves Dousman, Wales, Genesee, Ottawa, and surrounding areas. Kettle Moraine High School is consistently rated among the stronger Wisconsin public high schools, with strong academic and extracurricular programs. Multiple elementary feeders include Dousman Elementary, Magee Elementary, and others depending on neighborhood.

For private school options, University Lake School in Hartland (K-12) and various Catholic schools in Waukesha and Oconomowoc are within reasonable distance.

Dousman Lifestyle

Kettle Moraine State Forest Southern Unit is the dominant outdoor draw. Hiking trails, mountain biking, cross-country skiing in winter, the Ice Age National Scenic Trail. Many Dousman residents bought specifically for this access.

Dousman Lions Derby Days is the village's biggest annual event, drawing thousands. Strong community feel.

Easy commute paths to Milwaukee (I-94), Madison (I-94 west), Waukesha (Highway 18), and Oconomowoc (Highway 67). Dousman residents typically have employer flexibility because the commute is manageable in any direction.
