Why Ixonia

Ixonia is what Lake Country was 30 years ago. Country roads, working farms, larger residential lots, fewer subdivisions, and a quieter daily pace. The town sits in Jefferson County just west of the Waukesha County line, which puts it right next to the Lake Country recreation amenities while keeping property taxes and density lower than most of Waukesha County.

For buyers, Ixonia is one of the most affordable ways to get acreage near the Lake Country. For sellers, Ixonia properties tend to attract a specific buyer type: someone who wants space, character, and proximity to recreation but does not need a Lake Country zip code on their address.

Ixonia Real Estate Market

Smaller residential lots in the village area typically range from $250K to $400K. Larger lot homes (1+ acre) typically range from $350K to $600K, often with outbuildings and rural character. Hobby farm and acreage properties can run from $500K to $1M depending on land size, water access, and condition.

Recent Sue Patti Group sale in Ixonia: the LT2 Rockvale Road land/property closed at $190K in September 2024. Land sales like this are typical of the Ixonia market — buyers planning to build or invest in raw acreage, not just buying existing homes.

What buyers ask us most

"Can I have animals on this property?" Most Ixonia parcels with sufficient acreage permit horses, chickens, and other small livestock under Jefferson County zoning. Specifics vary by parcel. We confirm before showing properties to buyers with this requirement.

Ixonia Schools

Ixonia is part of the Oconomowoc Area School District, which is somewhat unusual for a Jefferson County community — most towns this far west are in different districts. Ixonia families benefit from access to Oconomowoc High School, Oconomowoc Middle School, and the strong elementary feeders. This is one of the practical advantages of buying in Ixonia: Oconomowoc-quality schools at Jefferson County prices.

For private school options, the same Lake Country choices apply: University Lake School, St. Jerome Catholic, and others within reasonable commute.

Ixonia Lifestyle

Country pace. Ixonia residents typically value quiet over convenience, space over square footage. Many buyers come here specifically because they grew tired of subdivision living.

Easy I-94 access. Despite the country feel, Ixonia is a 5-minute drive to I-94, which means Milwaukee, Waukesha, and Madison commutes are all manageable. This is unusual for a community this rural.

Growing slowly. Ixonia has been gradually adding population without losing character. Some new construction, but most of the housing stock remains established homes on larger parcels.
